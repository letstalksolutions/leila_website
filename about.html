About Me

Leila Hawkins

Journalist, Editor & Mentor

I'm a London-based journalist and editor specializing in human rights, social justice, and culture. With over a decade of experience in the field, I've contributed to publications including The Guardian, VICE, Refinery29, and Metro UK.

As co-founder of NADJA Media, a digital news platform highlighting women's achievements and contributions, I've worked to address the gender imbalance in media representation, where only 25% of news stories worldwide feature women as experts and spokespeople.

My passion for ethical journalism and media literacy has led me to focus on training and mentoring aspiring journalists, equipping them with the skills to navigate today's complex media landscape with integrity and purpose.

My Mission

In a world where 94% of people in the UK have witnessed misinformation on social media, and at least 46% of children aged 12 to 15 find it difficult to identify false news stories, media literacy has never been more important.

Research has shown that exposure to misinformation is linked to negative health outcomes including anxiety and poor mental health. A recent study found that young people are more likely to trust social media influencers than verified sources, and teachers often lack the confidence and training to address online misinformation.

My mission is to address this critical gap by providing high-quality journalism training that equips the next generation with the skills to:

  • Critically analyze social media and news content
  • Identify misinformation and disinformation
  • Verify sources and detect bias in reporting
  • Create ethical, impactful journalism
  • Develop confidence in their ideas and abilities

Through NADJA Media, I'm also committed to addressing gender imbalance in journalism, highlighting women's achievements and contributions in a media landscape where female voices are often underrepresented.

"Media literacy is crucial, and our expertise and commitment to editorial integrity makes us ideally placed to support teachers to tackle the growing challenges of misinformation, and help young people sharpen their critical thinking and storytelling skills."
